Abstract

Text-to-video diffusion models have made significant progress in recent years, yet there has been no dedicated research on generating fire scene videos with physical realism and visual fidelity. To address this gap, we present T2VFire (Text-to-Video Fire Scene Generation), the first model specifically designed for fire scene video generation. This training-free framework integrates visual language model (VLM) agents, Blender’s physics engine, and text-to-image diffusion models to enhance video synthesis quality. T2VFire employs a fine-tuned VLM agent and Blender in an iterative process to create scene components that align with textual descriptions and fire dynamics, which are then used as prompts for Stable Diffusion XL (SDXL) to generate videos. Additionally, the model incorporates a cross-frame attention mechanism and convex combination of background masks to improve temporal consistency across frames. Experimental results demonstrate that T2VFire excels at producing highquality fire scene videos, establishing a new paradigm for video generation in virtual and augmented reality, while also supporting smart firefighting and the development of digital twin systems for building fire safety management.
